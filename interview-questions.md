# Interview Questions

This document collects **classic interview questions** that are asked across most tech companies,  
especially for software engineering / systems roles.

It is intentionally **high-level and non-technical**.  
Detailed technical questions live in the corresponding sub-repositories.

---

## 1. Introduction & Background

### Present yourself
- Who are you professionally?
- What is your current role / last role?
- What kind of problems have you been working on recently?

Focus on:
- Your **core expertise**
- The **type of systems** you’ve worked on
- The **impact** you had

---

### Past experience & missions
- Describe your previous missions and responsibilities
- Highlight projects that are relevant to the role you’re applying for

Emphasize:
- Scale (users, data, latency, throughput)
- Constraints (performance, reliability, deadlines)
- Technologies that match the target role (C++, Linux, low-latency, distributed systems, etc.)

---

## 2. Company & Motivation

### What do you know about the company?
You should be able to explain:
- What the company does
- Its domain (finance, SaaS, infra, trading, etc.)
- How it makes money
- Who its customers are

Optional but strong signals:
- Team size
- Countries / markets where it operates
- Competitors
- Recent news or products

---

### Why do you want to join this company?
- Why this company and not another?
- Why this domain?
- Why now?

Good answers connect:
- Your background
- Their problems
- Your long-term interests

---

### Why are you the right fit?
- What problem can *you* solve for them?
- What do you bring that is hard to find?
- How does your experience reduce their risk?

---

## 3. Problem Solving & Thinking

### Describe something difficult you worked on
- What made it difficult?
- What were the constraints?
- What went wrong?
- How did you solve it?

Interviewers look for:
- Structured thinking
- Ownership
- Tradeoffs
- Learning from failure

---

### Explain something complicated
- Explain a complex technical topic you know well
- Adapt your explanation to a non-expert audience

This tests:
- Clarity of thought
- Ability to teach
- Depth of understanding

---

### How would you debug an issue in production?
Typical follow-ups:
- What if logs are missing?
- What if the issue is intermittent?
- What if latency spikes only under load?

Look for:
- Methodical approach
- Observability mindset
- Risk management
- Communication during incidents

---

## 4. Strengths & Weaknesses

### What are your strongest points?
Good answers are:
- Concrete
- Backed by examples
- Relevant to the role

Examples:
- Performance optimization
- System-level debugging
- Ownership
- Reliability mindset

---

### What are your weakest points?
Avoid clichés.

Good answers:
- Show self-awareness
- Show improvement over time
- Do not undermine core job requirements

---

## 5. Collaboration & Work Style

### How do you handle disagreements?
- Technical disagreements
- Design reviews
- Conflicting priorities

---

### How do you work with non-technical stakeholders?
- Product
- Management
- Sales / clients

---

### How do you handle pressure and deadlines?
- Production incidents
- Tight releases
- High-stakes situations

---

## 6. Growth & Learning

### How do you keep your skills up to date?
- Books
- Talks
- Side projects
- Reading code / specs

---

### Describe a failure and what you learned from it
Focus on:
- Responsibility
- Learning
- Change in behavior

---

## 7. Closing Questions

### Do you have questions for us?
You should always have some.

Examples:
- How is success measured in this role?
- What are the biggest technical challenges today?
- What does the first 6 months look like?
- How do incidents and on-call work?
